Page 1

A tiny seed fell from a bird's beak onto cracked earth in Lagos. The sun beat down. Cars roared. No one noticed. But a drop of unexpected rain softened the soil. Slowly, a sprout emerged, a fragile green against the harsh grey. It stretched towards the light, a silent promise of life in the heart of the city.

Page 2

Iya Risikat wove vibrant patterns on her loom in Makoko. Each color held a memory: the blue of the lagoon, the yellow of the sun, the red of a market day. As her fingers danced, she hummed a low song, a melody passed down through generations. The threads intertwined, telling a silent story of resilience and beauty amidst the water.
